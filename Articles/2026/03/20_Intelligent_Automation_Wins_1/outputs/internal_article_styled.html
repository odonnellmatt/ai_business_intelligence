Why This Matters Right Now

JPMorgan has been processing 12,000 commercial loan agreements annually through its COiN system since 2017 — work that previously consumed 360,000 legal and loan officer hours per year. That is not a projection or a pilot result. It is eight years of verified operational reality.

While many organisations are still debating whether intelligent automation is ready for serious deployment, a cohort of enterprises has moved past debate into scale, and the distance between them and the field is widening.

What the Research Shows

The evidence base across financial services, manufacturing, logistics, and telecoms is now substantive enough to draw clear conclusions — not about AI in the abstract, but about specific tools, specific companies, and specific problems that were genuinely broken before.

At Aviva, manual claims liability assessment was inconsistent and slow — complex cases took weeks longer than necessary, routing errors sent claims to the wrong specialisms, and complaint volumes were high. After deploying 80 AI models in partnership with McKinsey QuantumBlack, the insurer reduced complex liability assessment time by 23 days per case, improved routing accuracy by 30%, and cut customer complaints by 65%. The documented savings of £60 million (approximately AU$115 million) are investor-disclosed — the highest-confidence single-organisation ROI figure in the research base.

IBM's Client Zero programme deployed 155 AI use cases across HR, IT, procurement, finance, and sales — using its own 175-country operation as the test environment. IBM's AskIT reduced standard IT support tickets by 56% between 2022 and 2024, with 86% of IT queries now resolved without human involvement. AskHR resolves 94% of HR queries autonomously. IBM states US$4.5 billion in annual run-rate productivity savings — IBM-stated rather than independently audited, but reflecting 3.9 million hours saved in 2024 alone.

The Failure Picture — Equally Clear

Ernst & Young research found that up to 50% of initial RPA projects fail — not due to technology limitations, but due to wrong process selection, insufficient change management, and underestimating bot fragility when application interfaces change. McKinsey's November 2025 State of AI survey (n=1,993) found that while 88% of organisations deploy AI in at least one function, only 6% can attribute more than 5% of EBIT to AI. Adoption is widespread; value is concentrated.

The Business Value — Translated

A vendor-commissioned Forrester Total Economic Impact study on SS&C Blue Prism modelled a US$4 billion revenue financial services organisation achieving a 330% three-year ROI and US$53.4 million NPV, with customer onboarding cut from 16–18 days to 2 days and payback under six months. This is a composite model and should be read as directional — but the underlying drivers (labour cost reduction, cycle time compression, error rate improvement) are the same mechanisms visible in named-company results.

What to Watch

01
Platform AI agent embedding (end of 2026). Gartner projects 40% of enterprise applications will have embedded AI agents by end of 2026, up from less than 5% in 2025. This is a product roadmap signal from Salesforce, ServiceNow, SAP, and Microsoft — it should inform your current platform renewal and licensing decisions, not just your AI strategy.
02
EU AI Act enforcement begins. Now in full effect, with penalties up to €35 million or 7% of global revenue for non-compliant high-risk AI systems in credit, employment, and insurance. For organisations with EU exposure, this is a direct, quantifiable cost of non-compliance — no longer hypothetical.
03
Agentic AI cancellation rate. Forrester predicts fewer than 15% of firms will activate agentic automation features by end of 2026. Gartner puts cancellation risk above 40% of fully autonomous agentic projects by 2027. The lower-risk near-term path is AI-assisted deterministic workflows — not fully autonomous multi-step agents.

Suggested Internal Next Steps

1
Commission a process diagnostic session with your Operations and Finance leads — ideally half a day — to map the five highest-volume, most repetitive processes each function manages. Cross-reference against Celonis process mining or your current ERP's native analytics to identify where those processes deviate, loop, or break. This is the prerequisite work that McKinsey identifies as the differentiator between high-performing and average automation programmes.
2
Request AI feature briefings from your existing platform vendors — Salesforce, ServiceNow, or Microsoft 365 account teams — specifically on AI features available within your current licence agreements, before your next renewal cycle. Many organisations are paying for AI capability they have not activated. Prioritise activation of existing tools over new platform procurement.
3
Assign one named person in IT or a governance function to map which existing automation programmes — including any RPA bots already running — have documented measurement frameworks in place. Gartner found fewer than 20% of companies excel at measuring hyperautomation effectiveness. Without measurement, you cannot make evidence-based decisions about where to continue, scale, or retire automation investment.
