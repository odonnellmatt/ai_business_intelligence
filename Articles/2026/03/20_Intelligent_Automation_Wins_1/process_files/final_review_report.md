## Full "Circulate Internally" Assessment

### Immediate relevance to business
The opening section establishes the competitive gap argument in concrete terms — eight years of verified JPMorgan COiN operation, the McKinsey 6% high-performer concentration figure, and the process-redesign-as-differentiator finding from a 1,993-respondent survey. A functional lead reading this understands both the scale of the opportunity and the structural reason why most organisations are failing to realise it.

### Actionability of function-specific ideas
All five function blocks pass the delegation test. Each contains:
- A named company with a specific before/after problem
- A named AI tool (not category)
- Specific measured outcomes with appropriate confidence flags
- A concrete first step requiring no new budget (diagnostic work, licence activation request, ticket data analysis)

The first-step design is particularly strong: none of the five first steps requires a procurement decision. They are designed to generate decision-making data, which is the appropriate first action for an organisation at early-to-mid adoption stage.

### Calibration to audience
The article avoids both the "AI will solve everything" and "AI is overhyped" poles. It presents an honest adoption-value gap (88% deploy AI, only 6% attribute >5% EBIT) alongside genuine scale examples (IBM US$4.5B, Aviva £60M). This calibration is appropriate for a sophisticated internal audience that has been through previous enterprise technology cycles and is alert to inflated claims.

### Framing of business value
Three-mechanism framing (labour deflection, quality improvement, cycle time compression) is appropriate for a cross-functional internal audience. The Gartner platform convergence signal is correctly oriented to procurement/renewal decisions. EU AI Act penalties are quantified. The workforce anxiety point (PwC 51% motivation gap) is absent from the article — correctly omitted given the instruction focus on operational wins, though it remains in the research summary for future reference.
